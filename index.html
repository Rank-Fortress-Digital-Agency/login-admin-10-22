Is my ISP providing me the connection speed claimed?


When using our Free Speed Testing Service you will see this data:

Download Speed - This is probably what you are interested in. It shows you how fast(at what speed in Mb/s) you can get information from the internet. This is also the number your Internet Service Provider is probably advertising about.

Upload Speed - How fast (at what speed in Mb/s) data is being transferred from your devices to the internet.

Latency/Ping - Also known as latency. It represents the time it takes for one data package to travel from a place on the internet to your computer.

Jitter - It is measuring the variations in the latency tests.

What is considered fast internet speed connection?


Nowadays, any speed around 100 Mbps is considered to be "fast" but not extremely fast but it is above average for most internet users.

100 Mbps internet speed should be powerful enough to stream, play games, and participate in online Zoom conferences.

What internet speed do I need for Netflix?


According to netflix.com website you need a connection speed of at least 5 megabits per second. Video quality set to Auto or High.

Is 50 Mbps internet speed fast enough to work from home?


50 Mbps speed in regular working conditions should be enough for working from home.

100 Mbps is a great choice if you have to work with huge files that need to be uploaded or downloaded.

200 Mbps is a great speed for 3 to 5 people using the internet in the same time.

What is a good internet speed for gaming?


When it comes to playing online games speed is essential but more important is to have a low latency, no higher that 20 milliseconds.

A minimum internet speed for casual gaming is somewhere around 3-6 Mbps but for competitive gaming, you'll want 25 Mbps or more.

Do WiFi routers affect internet speed?


Routers can affect your internet speed if the maximum wireless speed it can handle is lower that the speed you get from your ISP.

Another this that can impact your internet speed is the simultaneous connections that the router in handling.
